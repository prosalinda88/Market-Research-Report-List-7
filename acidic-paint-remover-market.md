<p><p>The acidic paint remover market is divided into two main types: oily and paste formulations. Oily paint removers are typically liquid-based, providing a quick penetration into surfaces to dissolve paint effectively. They are ideal for intricate areas but may require more careful application. Paste removers, on the other hand, have a thicker consistency, allowing for better adhesion to vertical surfaces and prolonged contact time with paint. Both types cater to different user preferences and application scenarios, enhancing their versatility in paint removal tasks.</p></p>
