<p><p>The X-ray tube heat exchangers market features two primary types: rotating anode and stationary anode. Rotating anode types utilize a spinning target to distribute heat more evenly during operation, enhancing durability and performance during high-intensity imaging. In contrast, stationary anode types have a fixed target, making them simpler and often more cost-effective, but with limitations in heat management. Each type serves specific applications, influencing factors like imaging quality, equipment lifespan, and operational efficiency in medical and industrial settings.</p></p>
