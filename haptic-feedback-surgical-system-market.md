<p><p>The Haptic Feedback Surgical System market comprises two primary types: Kinesthetic Feedback Systems and Cutaneous Feedback Systems. Kinesthetic Feedback Systems replicate the sensation of force and resistance experienced during surgical procedures, allowing surgeons to feel the movement and tension of instruments. Cutaneous Feedback Systems, on the other hand, provide tactile sensations through the skin, enhancing the perception of surface textures and vibrations. Both systems aim to improve surgical precision and outcomes by offering enhanced sensory feedback during minimally invasive procedures.</p></p>
