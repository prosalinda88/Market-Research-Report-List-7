<p><p>The automotive drivetrain lubricant market is primarily categorized into fully synthetic and semisynthetic lubricants. Fully synthetic lubricants are engineered from chemically modified petroleum components or other base oils, offering superior performance, enhanced thermal stability, and improved protection against wear, making them ideal for high-performance vehicles. Semisynthetic lubricants combine synthetic and conventional oil components, providing a balance of cost-effectiveness and performance. They offer better protection and efficiency than conventional oils, appealing to a broader range of consumers seeking quality at a moderate price.</p></p>
