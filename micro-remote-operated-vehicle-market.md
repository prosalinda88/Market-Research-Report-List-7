<p><p>The Micro Remote Operated Vehicle (ROV) market is categorized into five classes based on functionality. Class I vehicles focus solely on observation tasks, offering basic video feeds. Class II adds payload options for limited manipulations alongside observation. Class III vehicles are work-class ROVs designed for complex operations, such as heavy lifting and tooling. Class IV vehicles are specialized for seabed interventions, capable of performing specific tasks on the ocean floor. Class V encompasses prototype or developmental vehicles, often used for experimental purposes.</p></p>
