<p><p>Wireless antennas play a crucial role in electronic devices, enabling effective communication across various frequency ranges. UHF (Ultra High Frequency) antennas operate within 300 MHz to 3 GHz, commonly used in televisions, mobile phones, and wireless networks, providing a balance of range and bandwidth. In contrast, VHF (Very High Frequency) antennas, operating between 30 MHz and 300 MHz, are often utilized in radio broadcasting, marine communications, and public safety systems, favoring longer transmission distances. The market for both types highlights their significance in modern connectivity.</p></p>
