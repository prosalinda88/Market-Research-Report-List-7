<p><p>Starch processing enzymes are vital for converting starch into sugars for various industrial applications. Alpha-amylase acts on starch to produce oligosaccharides and dextrins, facilitating liquefaction in food and beverage industries. Beta-amylase further breaks down these products into maltose, which is essential in brewing and baking. Amyloglucosidase completes the process by hydrolyzing dextrins into glucose, valuable for sweeteners and fermentation. Other enzymes in this market include pullulanase and glucoamylase, each serving specific functions in starch breakdown and modification processes.</p></p>
